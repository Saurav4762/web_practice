HTML Frames (Historical Context)

While HTML5 doesn't support frames (<frameset> and <frame>) due to accessibility and usability issues, they were commonly used in early web development to divide a page into multiple sections. Here's an example of how they were structured:

<frameset cols="25%,*,25%">
  <frame src="menu.html" name="menu">
  <frame src="content.html" name="content">
  <frame src="ads.html" name="ads">
</frameset>

Modern web development uses <iframe> for embedding external content and CSS/JavaScript for creating responsive layouts instead of frames.
